Welcome

Welcome to E-101. A website that teaches students how to study. In this website, I will be providing users a few methods and tricks & tips on how to effectively and efficiently study.

Absorbing Information

The first skill one must know is how to even take in the information he/she is receiving. The most famous and recommended method is Active Recall.

This method includes listening to your teacher for a few seconds and writing a summary of what he/she said. As an example I will use two students: John and Bob.

John uses the active recall method and summarizes the information given by keeping what's important and the other non-important information he doesn't use. This boosts his productivity rate by alot and allows him to study faster while still scoring good grades.
Unlike John, Bob doesn't use active recall. Instead he just writes down all his information and attempts to study after. Due to the amount of information he wrote down, he has a hard time studying and often either loses all that he studied in no time, or end up feeling really stressed and overloaded, or sometimes even both.

Note-Taking Methods

The second skill you can learn is how to document the summarized information. There are many ways but I shall provide you with my top 3 note taking methods. They include Cornell Method, Charting Method, and Outline Method.

Cornell Method

This method includes having a section for main notes, a section for key tips / questions and a section for a summary of the main notes. It is a broad and versatile method capable of being used in nearly all subjects.

Charting Method

The Charting Method is a table-like structure to document notes categorized into topics. For each topic you have a description, observation and sometimes even a pros and cons section. The description serves as a box to store the notes of that topic while the observation serves as a box to store summarized notes or, as the name says, observations. The pros and cons tells the goods and bads about a specific topic. This method can be used in many subjects but is mainly used in The Sciences.

Outline Method

The outline method includes making a topic and placing the subtopic under, then placing notes about that subtopic under the subtopic in a neat, indented format. It can be used in almost all subjects and is very useful and effective.

Studying The Notes

Now that we finished how to summarize the notes and how to note down tha summarized info, we shall go over how to study the notes. Actually studying the notes is the hard part but if yoi follow these tips it will be as easy as saying ABC.

Tip 1- Repitition:
Repitition is essential - as a matter of fact, it is the key to studying. Repitition involves repeating something over and over again 'til it's stuck in your brain. eg:

Tip 2- Make a Study Timetable:
A study timetable is essential in studying as you can manage the times you study with ease. It is recommended that in your study timetable you have:
  • All your subjects
  • Around 20 - 45 minutes of studying.
  • Break Time

Tip 3- Use Flashcards
For our very last tip I recommend flashcards. Flashcards are a great help when it comes to studying as they require you to peak in your brain for the answer to the question. You can make flashcards irl by taking a piece of paper or coloured paper and writing a question on one side and the answer on the other. You can also do it online through apps such as:
  • Quizlet
  • Gizmo
  • Flashcard Maker
